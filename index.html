What They Do

The AWS of Emerging Market Payments

DLocal is a payment technology platform connecting global enterprises — think Netflix, Amazon, Uber, Spotify, Microsoft, and Booking.com — to local payment infrastructure across 44+ emerging markets in Latin America, Africa, and Asia.

Its value proposition is simple but profound: a merchant that wants to accept a Brazilian Pix payment, a Nigerian bank transfer, or an Indian UPI payment doesn't have to build relationships with 44 local banks, navigate 37 regulatory regimes, and manage 900+ local payment methods. They call DLocal.

Business model: asset-light take rate on Total Payment Volume (TPV). Near-zero capex, high FCF conversion, and negative working capital in steady state. Think Visa/Mastercard economics applied to markets where Visa/Mastercard can't reach.

Five Layers of Durable Moat

DLocal's competitive position deepens with every transaction processed — scale, licenses, and data create compounding barriers that take years to replicate.

🏛️
Regulatory Licensing
37 payment licenses across 26 markets — each taking 12–36 months to obtain. 16 additional applications in process, including the United States. Licenses are non-transferable barriers to entry.

Single-Integration Network Effects
One API covering 900+ local payment methods across 44 markets. Merchants integrate once, then expand globally. Deep integration in merchant tech stacks creates high switching costs.
📊
Data & Conversion Intelligence
Processing 3.5B+ transactions annually (6,700 per minute) generates proprietary fraud models, conversion optimization, and smart routing logic that competitors cannot replicate without scale.
🤝
Merchant Wallet Expansion
Top 50 merchants served across 12 countries and 50 payment methods on average. NRR of 145% proves merchants spend more over time. TPV Retention reached 158% in 2025.
💰
Zero-Debt Balance Sheet
$720M in net cash. Zero debt across its entire 10-year history. Asset-light model with <0.2% capex-to-revenue. This financial fortress funds R&D and buybacks while competitors leverage up.
⚡
First-Mover Depth in Complex Markets
Deep local relationships, local banking partnerships, and operational knowledge in markets like Nigeria, Egypt, Argentina, and India that took years to build. Scale drives better FX liquidity and pricing.

Bull Case vs. Bear Case

🐂 Bull Case — Why DLO Wins
✓
FIFA World Cup 2026 is a unique macro tailwind DLocal operates in all three host nations (USA, Canada, Mexico). Cross-border digital commerce across LatAm will surge. This is a 2026-specific catalyst that few companies in the world can monetize as directly.
✓
$300M buyback at historically depressed valuation Stock is down 61.6% from IPO despite 45% revenue CAGR. Buyback at 3x EV/Rev is extremely accretive — management is buying back stock at a fraction of its intrinsic value.
✓
NRR of 145% means the base grows automatically Without adding a single new merchant, DLO's revenue would grow 45% per year from existing customers expanding into new countries and payment methods. The growth is structurally embedded.
✓
TAM still essentially untapped DLO holds <2% of a $2T+ TAM that is expected to double by 2030. Share of wallet with existing merchants is only ~10%. Decades of runway ahead.
✓
New products are high-margin and defensible BNPL (Fuse) and stablecoins carry higher take rates than core payment processing. 88% QoQ BNPL growth in Q4 2025 suggests rapid merchant adoption. These are additive revenue streams, not replacements.
✓
Goldman, JPMorgan, and Citi all back the long Goldman ($19), JPMorgan ($18), Citi ($17) — the top three sell-side banks all have Buy ratings. Institutional conviction is high and the stock is under-owned.
🐻 Bear Case — What Could Go Wrong
✗
Take rate compression is structural Take rate has declined from 4.1% in 2021 to 2.7% in 2025 as large merchants negotiate volume discounts. If this continues faster than expected, revenue growth will lag TPV growth. Management guided lower gross profit margins for 2026.
✗
Merchant concentration risk Top 10 merchants still represent a disproportionate share of revenue. Loss of a major client (e.g., a streamer pulling LatAm volumes) could materially impact quarterly results and trigger negative price action.
✗
EM currency & macro volatility Argentina, Egypt, Nigeria, and other key markets have currency instability risks. FX headwinds directly compress dollar-reported revenues. Argentine peso devaluation hurt Q4 2025 gross profit despite strong volumes.
✗
Regulatory risk is ever-present Operating in 44 markets means constant exposure to regulatory change. A license revocation, capital controls, or new data localization requirements in Brazil or Mexico could be material disruptions.
✗
Competition from local champions and Stripe Local players (StoneCo, PagSeguro, MercadoPago) dominate domestic markets. Stripe is expanding internationally. A well-funded competitor winning a global merchant could displace DLO in key verticals.
✗
Stock has never recovered from 2021 IPO high DLO IPO'd at ~$32, reached $60+, and is now at $12.86. Multiple compression post-IPO has been severe. A re-rating requires sustained earnings beats and macro stability — not guaranteed.
